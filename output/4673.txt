``                            DIS 55&#xa0;The Women’s Refugee Commission’s (WRC) seeks to advance the rights and dignity of persons with disabilities affected by crisis and conflict, through advocacy, research and capacity development initiatives to support disability-inclusive refugee policy and practice at international and country levels.The WRC commends the UK Government Department for International Development (DFID) for its commitment to persons with disabilities affected by crisis and conflict, most notably recent support to Syrian refugees with disabilities in Jordan, as well as wider commitments to advancing the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD) through support to partner governments and capacity building for disabled people’s organizations (DPOs) in developing countries.Recommendations to the International Development Committee to further advance disability inclusion across the wider humanitarian sector include:

[[[(i)]]]&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; 

[[[ Expand understanding of ‘vulnerability’ in the humanitarian sector]]] by supporting systematic reviews of available literature; conducting evaluations of prioritized and targeted assistance, identifying advantages and disadvantages of different methodologies in a variety of humanitarian contexts; and  promoting sharing and learning about these different methodologies across the humanitarian sector.&#xa0;
