[[[Executive summary:]]]As a past president of the Primate Society of Great Britain, a scientific body established for the promotion of research into primate biology, conservation, and captive care, I would like to provide some comments for this enquiry. My expertise is not with regard to the numbers of primates held privately, but rather with the fundamental issues of the adequacy of the protection of primate welfare for pet primates and the appropriateness of the keeping of primates as “pets”. &#xa0;As a result of my 39 years of experience with non-human primate biology, behaviour and conservation both in the wild and captivity, I can perceive no ethical, husbandry, conservation or historical justifications for continuing to allow the keeping of primates as companion animals in the UK.&#xa0;
