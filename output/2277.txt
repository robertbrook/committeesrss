[[[Executive Summary and Recommendations]]]&#xa0;-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; The EITI has the potential to positively impact the UK extractive industries; we recommend that the Government gives due consideration to how its principles can be tailored to the specific domestic context and in doing so demonstrate the importance of national-level ownership and adaption.-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; The impact of EITI implementation will depend on adherence to optional elements.  We recommend the Government provides data in an accessible format with sufficient contextual information. Contracts regulating extraction should also be published. -&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; The Government should consider embedding EITI obligations into existing governance structures where possible, rather than creating additional layers of reporting.-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; The extractive industries pose a significant reputational risk to the UK as a centre for global business.  Particular concerns are raised by the use of nominee directors and opaque corporate structures.  Regulation of both the London Stock Exchange and the Alternative Investment Market has proven insufficient and should be strengthened. -&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; By creating a public register of beneficial ownership the Government will develop a more transparent business environment and help prevent the laundering of illegitimately raised natural resource assets, often from conflict or weak governance states.  -&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; The Government should ensure enforcement mechanisms have sufficient resources and expertise to realise our strong anti-bribery legislation.
