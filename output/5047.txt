[[[&#xa0;]]]I, Matthew Ellis Police and Crime Commissioner for Staffordshire give notice that&#xa0;I have set out below under the appropriate headings my interests which I am required to declare.  I have put “none” where I have no such interests under any heading. &#xa0;I recognise that it can be a criminal offence to:&#xa0;(i) omit the information which ought to be given in this notice:&#xa0;(ii) provide information which is materially false or misleading;&#xa0;(iii) fail to give further notices in order to:&#xa0;- bring up to date information given in this notice&#xa0;             - declare an interest which I acquire after the date of this notice and have to                declare.&#xa0;In this document:&#xa0;“body in which the relevant person has a beneficial interest” means a firm in which the relevant person is a partner or a body corporate of which the relevant person is a director, or in the securities of which the relevant person has a beneficial interest;&#xa0;“director” includes a member of the committee of management of an industrial and provident society;&#xa0;“land” includes an easement, servitude, interest or right in or over land which does not carry with it a right for the relevant person (alone or jointly with another) to occupy the land or to receive income;&#xa0; “elected local policing body” means the elected Local Policing Body to which the relevant person has been elected.&#xa0;“relevant period” means the period of 12 months ending with the day on which the relevant person makes an entry amendment or deletion to the register of disclosable interests.&#xa0;“relevant person” means the person(s) who is/are elected/appointed Police and Crime Commissioner and Deputy Police and Crime Commissioner.&#xa0;“securities” means shares, debentures, debenture stock, loan stock, bonds, units of a collective investment scheme within the meaning of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000(b) and other securities of any description, other than money deposited with a building society.&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;
