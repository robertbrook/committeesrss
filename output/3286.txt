[[[&#xa0;]]]&#xa0;&#xa0;1.              The Planning Inspectorate (PINs):&#xa0;1.1&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; PINs is a Government Agency jointly sponsored by DCLG and Welsh Government dealing with planning appeals and related matters in the two jurisdictions. Other than in respect of Nationally Significant Infrastructure Projects (NSIP) falling within the purview of the Planning Act 2008, the operation of the planning system is devolved to the Welsh Ministers.  &#xa0;1.2&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Planning policy produced by Welsh Ministers and Local Planning Authorities (LPA) informs appeal decisions made by Planning Inspectors. &#xa0;&#xa0;1.3&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Planning permission is required for development comprising “the carrying out of building, engineering, mining or other operations in, on, over or under land, or the making of any material change of use in the use of the buildings or other land” (S.55 TCPA 1990)&#xa0;1.4&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Planning applications for development relating to shale gas projects are made to the relevant LPA. As Unitary Authorities, their responsibilities include those of a Mineral Planning Authority.&#xa0;2.1&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Applications fall into two distinct categories: those projects relating to plant and operations associated with exploration and testing and secondly, projects associated with any extraction. &#xa0;2.2&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; The LPA determines whether an Environmental Statement is required under the EIA Regulations 1999 (SI 199 No 293). The Regulations specify two separate lists of projects:Schedule 1 projects: an EIA is required in every case;Schedule 2 projects: an EIA is required only if the project is judged likely to give rise to significant environmental effects.&#xa0;2.3&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Projects relating to shale gas exploration and/or extraction would be likely to fall within Schedule 2.&#xa0;2.4&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; In addition to standard consultations including Natural Resources Wales (NRW -successor to the Environment Agency in Wales) in respect of mineral applications, the LPA must notify the Coal Authority if the land contains coal, and/or DECC if it contains gas or oil. (GDPO Art 17, SI 2012/801)&#xa0;2.5&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Applications refused planning permission by an LPA, or granted subject to allegedly unacceptable conditions, or where no decision has been made within the appropriate period (see GDPO Art 22 and SI 1999/293) can, within a period of 6 months, be appealed to the Welsh Ministers (S.78 of the TCPA 1990).&#xa0;2.6&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Under s.74 of the TCPA, the Welsh Ministers may issue a ‘direction’ restricting the grant of planning permission by an LPA and under s.77 can ‘call-in’ applications for their own determination. Through s.79 Welsh Ministers appoint Planning Inspectors to conduct any inquiry and then to submit a report recommending the suggested outcome. &#xa0;&#xa0;3.1&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; S.38(6) PCA 2004 states “If regard is to be had to the development plan for the purpose of any determination to be made under the Planning Acts the determination must be made in accordance with the plan unless material considerations indicate otherwise”. &#xa0;3.2&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; The development plan comprises adopted Structure Plans, Local Plans, Unitary Development Plans and Minerals Plans unless and until those Plans are superseded by Local Development Plans (LDP). Currently 10/25 LPAs in Wales have adopted LDPs.&#xa0;3.3&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; A LDP need not repeat national policy statements issued by Welsh Government. These comprise Planning Policy Wales 2010 (PPW), Minerals Planning Policy Wales 2000 (MPPW) and the associated Technical Advice Notes (TANs). These statements represent material considerations of significant weight.&#xa0;3.4&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; In respect of oil and gas operations, MPPW states at paragraph 64:-&#xa0;              Where oil and gas operations can be carried out in an environmentally acceptable way and consistent with the principles of sustainable development, there is no case in land use planning terms for placing more restrictions on the development than are necessary to ensure the protection of the environment. Development plans should indicate those areas where oil and gas operations are likely to be acceptable in principle subject to development control criteria being met in a particular case, as well as those areas where operations are unlikely to be acceptable. Policies should distinguish clearly between the three stages of exploration, appraisal and development (The Petroleum Act 1998). Mineral planning authorities should establish with the Department of Trade and Industry (now DECC) the areas which are licensed, and identify any environmental and other constraints on production and processing in those areas. The industry has an important role to play in making available to authorities information on their forward plans and the extent of known resources. The licence system (The Coal Act 1994) brought into effect in 1995 introduced a single licence, the Petroleum Exploration and Development Licence (PEDL) covering exploration, appraisal and developmental activity. Activities under such licences must be carried out in accordance with the requirements for planning permission. For coalbed methane, permission will be required also from the owner of the coal, usually the Coal Authority. It should be noted that off-shore oil and gas production is likely to require onshore installations that may require planning permission.&#xa0;3.5&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; MPPW also states in respect of:(a)&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Para 21: National Parks and AONB:- “Minerals development should not take place in these areas save in exceptional circumstances…..”.&#xa0;(b)&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Para 23:  Special Protection Areas; Special Areas of Conservation and Ramsar sites:- “Minerals proposals within or likely to significantly affect potential and classified SPAs, designated, candidate or proposed SACs or Ramsar sites must be carefully examined in relation to the site’s conservation objectives in order to ascertain whether or not they are likely to be significant in terms of the ecological objectives of the site”.&#xa0;(c)&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Para 25 :  SSSIs and National Nature Reserves:- “Minerals proposals within SSSIs or likely to affect them should be very carefully considered, and where the impact is likely to be significant they should be subject to the most rigorous examination, and the need for the mineral must be balanced against environmental and other relevant considerations”.&#xa0;3.6&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Planning Policy Wales (PPW) also states in respect of such sensitive areas:-Para 5.5.6: “In National Parks or AONBs, special considerations apply to major development proposals which are more national than local in character. Major developments should not take place in National Parks or AONBs except in exceptional circumstances. This may arise where, after rigorous examination, there is demonstrated to be an overriding public need and refusal would be severely detrimental to the local economy and there is no potential for locating the development elsewhere or meeting the need in some other way.&#xa0;3.7&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; PPW states that the effects of pollution on human health and the environment is relevant (Para 12.1.5); that planning and environmental management are separate but complementary and do not duplicate one another (Para 13.1.1); LPAs and NRW should thus work together to avoid or minimise risks (Para 12.7.2); Planning authorities should operate on the basis that the relevant pollutant control regimes will be properly applied and enforced by other agencies (13.10.2).               3.8&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; PPW and TAN 15 also provide a sequential, risk based approach to ensure that development is located away from areas at risk of flooding whenever possible. Risk assessments are often required to inform decisions and any requirements for mitigation measures. Likewise, PPW and TAN 5 provide policy guidance in respect of nature conservation and TAN 11 in relation to noise.&#xa0;&#xa0;4.1&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;
