[[[STOPAIDS ]]]Introduction&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; STOPAIDS is the network of 80 UK agencies working since 1986 to secure an effective global response to HIV and AIDS.&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; We endorse the submission from the Bond Disability and Development Working Group and make a few simple points bringing the perspective of a network focused on HIV and AIDS.&#xa0;Context&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Disabled people’s access to information and programmes addressing HIV and AIDS is often inadequate. This flows from a number of barriers and including: &#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; prevailing cultural norms which assume that disabled people do not have sexual identities and/or are not vulnerable to sexual violence which puts them at risk of HIV infection;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; the inability of mainstream development agencies to reach out to and communicate effectively with disabled people;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; the reluctance of influential agencies such as DFID to fully prioritise the rights of disabled people.&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Additionally, and for the first time since the start of the HIV epidemic 32 years ago, 10% of the adult population living with HIV in low- and middle-income countries is aged 50 or older. This brings an increased need to ensure equitable and sustainable access to affordable, innovative medicines in order to off-set the increased risks of more chronic illnesses and disability.&#xa0;&#xa0;Recommendations&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; In its recent publication, Towards Zero Infections – Two Years On, DFID identifies disabled people as being a ’hard to reach’ group. We therefore recommend that DFID articulate clear goals for how it will use its financial and political influence to advance the position of disabled people in the context of the HIV epidemic in order to (a) increase disabled people’s access to prevention, treatment, care and support and (b) reduce people’s vulnerability to disability resulting from a lack of equitable and sustainable access to affordable, innovative medicines.&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Drawing on the programmatic experience of our members we specifically recommend:&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; building the capacity of disabled people’s organisations to provide information on HIV/AIDS;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; encouraging the disaggregation of data in all reporting on prevention, treatment and care and support programmes;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; recognising the vulnerability of disabled people, and disabled women and girls in particular, to sexual violence which increases their vulnerability to HIV and AIDS;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; ensuring that health facilities are physically and linguistically accessible for disabled people, extending Lynne Featherstone’s recent commitment that all new school funded by DFID would be accessible to CWD and extending this to health centres;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; recognising the additional stigma and poverty facing disabled people who are also HIV positive.&#xa0;December 2013&#xa0; See UNAIDS HIV and Aging report, November 2013 http://www.unaids.org/en/media/unaids/contentassets/documents/unaidspublication/2013/20131101_JC2563_hiv-and-aging_en.pdf http://stopaids.org.uk/our-actions/access-to-medicines/ http://reliefweb.int/sites/reliefweb.int/files/resources/Towards%20zero%20infections%20two%20years%20on_0.pdf
