[[[ Land Access and Recreation Association (LARA) – Written evidence]]]

[[[Summary of Key Issues]]]•&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; We support the work and consensus recommendations of the Stakeholder Working Group, which underpin this part of the Bill. Our members are not&#xa0;directly&#xa0;affected by the proposals in sections 12-18 of the Bill and the associated Schedules, and we support the&#xa0;Stakeholder Working Group’s recommendations (only) as contained within the Bill.•&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; We are very concerned that groups such as the Green Lanes Environmental Action Movement (GLEAM) and the Peak District Green Lanes Association (PDGLA), each with a single-issue objective to restrict or destroy our members’ lawful activities, are making representations such that additional traffic regulation provisions are introduced by the Deregulation Bill, and/or that all unsealed public roads and byways open to all traffic are administratively downgraded to restricted byway status.•&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; These issues have not been before the Stakeholder Working Group, have not been through any process of public consultation, and are not objectively evidence-based. Far from being deregulatory, these proposals will operate to increase local authority and police burdens.•&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; LARA believes that there is no evidential case made for any of these proposals, that they will introduce an additional layer of regulation and burden, and that any change in this area of law and practice should first go through a proper process of public consultation.•&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Any countryside access organisation (including GLEAM and PDGLA) is able to bring such proposals directly to the Countryside Minister, and to the Rights of Way Review Committee, which is constituted and operated to receive and consider such matters and to make recommendations to the Government.•&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; In our respectful view, what is being proposed to the Committee is not deregulatory, and is simply an attempt to use the Deregulation Bill 2013 to circumvent a proper process of public consultation by the appropriate Government Department, and (if necessary) a full and proper process in Parliament.&#xa0;
