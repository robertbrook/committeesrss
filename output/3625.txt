&#xa0;Written evidence from TravelWatch NorthWest (HSR 017)&#xa0;

[[[HIGH SPEED RAIL]]]&#xa0;TravelWatch NorthWest (TWNW) is an independent Community Interest Company representing users of all forms of public transport in NW England. We support the concept of a high speed railway to connect London with the North West of England and have always expressed our strong concerns over the lack of a national-wide strategy for a high speed rail network to provide a context for decisions which will be being taken in the next few years. We will be responding in more detail to the DfT’s consultation on “HS2 Phase Two: proposed line of route from the West Midlands to Manchester and Leeds” in due course.&#xa0;We produced a position paper on HS2 in October 2009 and revised this in May 2011 when we responded to the HOCTC inquiry into the strategic case for High Speed rail. Both these documents are in your files. The basis of our views then is largely unchanged though circumstances have moved on.     &#xa0;In summary we see the main argument for HS2 as primarily being an increase in capacity to

[[[ ]]]meet growth beyond the capacity of the existing system and we also believe it will bring economic benefits to the north. However, it is vitally important that concentration on the HS2 project does not deprive the existing route network of investment or continuing development. A holistic view of HS2 as part of a new railway system needs to be taken.
