[[[Summary]]]Women's Aid is the national domestic violence charity that co-ordinates and supports an England-wide network of over 300 local services working to end domestic violence against women and children. Keeping the voices of survivors at the heart of its work, Women's Aid campaigns for better legal protection and services, providing a strategic "expert view" to government on laws, policy and practice affecting abused women and children. In partnership with its national network, Women's Aid runs public awareness and education campaigns, bringing together national and local action, and developing new training and resources.&#xa0;&#xa0; &#xa0;Women's Aid provides a package of vital 24 hour lifeline services through its publications (available in 11 languages including English) and running the Freephone 24 Hour National Domestic Violence Helpline in partnership with Refuge - 0808 2000 247.

[[[&#xa0;]]]Women’s Aid welcomes the opportunity to submit written evidence to the Work and Pensions Select Committee Inquiry into support for housing costs in the reformed welfare system and will restrict comments to our areas of expertise. 

[[[&#xa0;]]]This response focuses on the implications of changes for survivors of domestic violence and the treatment of housing costs within Universal Credit. &#xa0;
