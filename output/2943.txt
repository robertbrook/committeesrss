[[[Evidence]]]&#xa0;* CQC is not acting as an agent for improvement as required in their governing legislation;* CQC does not have sufficient expertise in the care home sector;* CQC’s strategy should build in the capability to inspect care homes at least twice a year;* performance measures for care homes should focus on improvement in the health, safety and well-being of residents;* there should be two inspections of care homes per year;* a focus on a few selected standards only is negligent;* specialists with expertise in care homes should be recruited to inspect care homes;* CQC appears to be taking steps to being a learning organisation, but they have a long way to go;* CQC could reduce the burdens on care homes for older people by becoming a reliable and trusted organisation;* CQC should carry out an evaluation of its priorities and inspection methodology;* CQCs regulatory regime should be specifically tailored to meet the needs of each sector;* the DH should improve its capability and expertise on the regulation of safety and quality; and* the CQC website should not allow care homes to expunge their (often poor) records by changing the name under which the provider is registered.-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; inspections remain too infrequent;-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; CQC’s inspection methodology remains inadequate;-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; enforcement action is lacking;-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; inspectors of care homes often lack expertise in the care home sector, in particular in care for older people;-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; inspection reports lack sufficient information for the public to judge how a home is performing and for prospective residents and their families to choose a home that will meet their needs.&#xa0;
