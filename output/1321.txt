&#xa0;Written evidence from Gloucestershire Rural Community Council (TIC 019)&#xa0;PARTNERS: Glos Rural Community Council, Forest of Dean District Council, Community Transport organisations, Glos County CouncilEvidence gathered for Big Lottery project to: -create a stronger and more sustainable community transport offer that will give access to social activities, community networks and services.-reduce the social and physical isolation of older, vulnerable and young people living in small rural settlements in the Forest of Dean through better information-enable knowledge sharing -develop relationships which enable more people in the community to work together on community transport solutions 

[[[Evidence  ]]]•&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; 58% of FOD population reside in small rurally isolated settlements with little or no public transport provision. 20% of residents in over half of the districts wards are over the age of 65 (Glos multi-agency MAiDEN data). •&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Accessibility data measuring travel time to 16 service destinations by driving, walking and public transport shows:- 70% of the district ranks in the 10% poorest access in the county- a quarter in the next 25% poorest access. - 80% of the Forest of Dean (FOD) is in the worst access quartile for public transport. (MAiDEN).•&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; 64% of respondents didn’t understand the Community Transport offer, coverage or how to use it.  (Bus Services Review December 2010)•&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Out of 41 parishes: five have 50% of the population over 50 years; fifteen parishes have 45% over 50 ( MAiDEN).

[[[Consultation]]]-lack of Public/ community transport leading to loneliness, isolation and depression. -older people become housebound/isolated when they physically/ financially cannot run a car-better information/alternative options would help people plan for the future. Forest Showcase Event attended by 3,500 people. Consulted 200 people. Detailed feedback from 66 people highlighting:  -&#xa0; access issues for young people outside school/training times(12); -&#xa0; transport in their village had been cut or was non-existent (16); -&#xa0; suggestions for enabling marginalised people – ie Dementia (2).  -&#xa0; don’t understand Community Transport, how it works, the cost, routes (36)-&#xa0; Gloucestershire Voices (user led self-advocacy organisation for adults with learning disabilities; their Forest Forum;  -&#xa0; Forest Pulse (independent charity which supports families with children with Special Needs – birth to nineteen) -&#xa0; Forest Youth Forum (6 people average) -&#xa0; Forest Youth Workers Network (37 local community organisations), -&#xa0; Forest Seniors Network (229 members) ,-&#xa0; Forest Health Forum (350 members evenly split between voluntary and statutory organisations/individuals) -&#xa0; Carers Partnership (Carers Glos, Alzheimer’s Society) 342 responses: 88% of people do not understand community transport; 62% how they can access it &amp; cost; 42%.routes
