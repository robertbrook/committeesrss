[[[ve industries in the 21st Century and is it in the UK’s interest to remain a global centre for them?]]]&#xa0;23.              It is in the UK’s strategic interests to secure sustainably the resources which support its population’s quality of life (including for food supply, drinking water, construction and energy); to capitalise on its competitive advantages in the extractive industries (some of which are referred to above) for economic benefit; and to ensure that industry has access to the critical and emerging resources it will need to be innovative and competitive.  The pressure on all geological resources will increase as the world’s population grows – this is a geopolitical matter, because deposits of these resources can only be found where nature has put them.  Some commodities trade globally, and for these the UK will be in competition with other countries.  Within the UK, some commodities (shale gas, for example) may occur in sensitive areas, and there is an opportunity to provide global leadership in securing social consent to operate.  To capitalise on our geographical and historical competitive advantages will require world-leading research and training. &#xa0;21 September 2013&#xa0;
