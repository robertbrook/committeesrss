[[[Universities and Colleges Union (WSC037)]]]IntroductionThis document suggests that a barrier to the careers of women in science in academia is the lack of long term funding leading to a lack of security of employment. The prospect of constantly looking for their next funded appointment is daunting to many women especially those trying to combine a career with raising a family.   In addition that the short term nature of research employment in academia leads to a lack of responsibility being shown by leaders of research for researchers’ career development which is detrimental to long term career prospects. It suggests ways in which the funding providers could address these issues. 

[[[&#xa0;]]]&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;Ensuring arrangements for the redeployment of staff either within the university or within the existing unit at the end of the project Providing bridging between projects – time can be spent writing papers, doing training or writing bids under guidance Appropriate time being given to training and development by timetabling it into activities or proving attendance at appropriate training courses – funders need to recognise this need in their funding modelsA statement of how CIs/PIs will demonstrate good practice should also form part of the award process – though without follow up on how this was implemented will be essential &#xa0;August 2013&#xa0;
