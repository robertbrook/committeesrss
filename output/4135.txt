[[[Summary]]]-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Many different kinds of contractors doing electrical work; much of that work is outside the scope of building regulations-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; “horses for courses” rather than “one size fits all” approach needed in defining Competence in a register-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; Register needs to be backed up by solid assessment scheme to raise it above the level of a “trade directory”, and assessment should be more than just the Part P scope where needed.&#xa0;Further to my letter of 5th October 2013 about registration schemes for electricians I have read more and talked to various people and would like to comment further.  My view has changed somewhat and I hope these new comments are of interest.&#xa0;Many contractors, home owners etc. work with electricity.  The central purpose of registration schemes is to approve the competence of individuals and businesses for Domestic new circuits and bathrooms (the scope of Part P).  This is a quite specific area in the overall landscape of electrical work and competence). In the mapping diagram attached it is just the purple shaded are in the centre.  This diagram shows that there are many other types of electrical work, premises and people doing that work and skills needed which are outside the scope of the part P registration schemes.  It is important to consider the whole picture rather than just the buildings regulation area.&#xa0;To a greater or lesser extent registration schemes approve competence in other areas as well – in my own assessments by Elecsa for example there has been a thorough review of all my electrical training, BS7671 requirements (not just part P) and also my business set up and practices.  This is what makes an Electrical Safety register stand out – that it is based on technical and business standards, rather than being a trade directory built only on recommendations.&#xa0;For the different kinds of electrical work from appliance delivery and fitters to full scale mech &amp; electrical maintenance businesses different levels of competence are needed – see examples on the mapping diagram attached.-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; The vast majority of full time electricians, such as myself, are registered with NICEIC or Elecsa backed up by Certsure, Electrical Safety Register, Electrical Safety Council and Electrical Contractors Association brands and organisations.   In the course of my day to day work I need a good working knowledge and where to look up all aspects of the regulations .&#xa0;-&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0; There are other groups of people such as kitchen fitters, appliance delivery/installers etc. who need some knowledge (eg. How to extend a ring main, check cable thickness, check the presence or absence of RCD protection) who do not need such a full working knowledge of the regulations and Part P to do their job, and it can sometimes be only a small part of their work.&#xa0;So I am no longer sure whether one register can give a competent person yes/no black and white definition.  It is “horses for courses” rather than “one size fits all” depending upon in which part of the mapping diagram the competent person is working.  The electrical industry seems more diverse than gas installation for example.  I still think there is no sense in having two registers with deceptively similar names, but there is at least a need to distinguish between full competence and specific/defined competence depending upon qualifications, demonstrated skills and line of business for individuals and their companies.  &#xa0;Overall homeowners, landlords, agents and other customers of businesses such as my own need to be able to find and be confident in good workmanship and to avoid rogue traders and poorly implemented DIY work.  &#xa0;December 2013&#xa0;&#xa0;EXAMPLE MAPPING OF STANDARDS AND SKILLS AGAINST TYPES OF PREMISES AND TYPES OF ELECTRICAL WORKER&#xa0;&#xa0;  BS 7671  &#xa0;Other Building RegsSpecial Skills&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;Part PBFL etceg. outdoor cable, design calcs, commercial, building work, plumbing&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;Types of Premises&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;&#xa0;Large Commercial (eg.
